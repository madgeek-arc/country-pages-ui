    What is the Country Page

    The OpenAIRE Country Pages map the diverse national Research and Development (R&D) landscapes in Europe to provide a 360o view on the Open Science maturity of more than 35 countries. The Country Pages compile information about Open Science policies, key actors, initiatives, digital infrastructure and repositories as well as on training and skills employed to realise them.

    Who is involved

    OpenAIRE maintains a strong network of experts known as the National Open Access Desks (NOADs) who are committed to supporting the adoption and implementation of Open Science in their national research environments. They nurture collaborations with national stakeholders and experts and leverage on those connections to populate, curate and update the content of their country pages on a regular basis.

    How information is gathered

    Two major sources that feed the Country Pages with text, numbers and figures are the NOADS with their national networks and the OpenAIRE Graph.

    Why is it important

    This service allows to trace the implementation of Open Science practices in different national settings and displays the potential for further development and collaboration in all areas of Open Science.

    What are the links with the ERA and EOSC

    The OpenAIRE Country Pages form the most complete and validated resource for Open Science in Europe complementing policy efforts and monitoring services, such as the Open Science Observatory.
